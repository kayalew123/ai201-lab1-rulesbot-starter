Source: UMD Dining Services - Frequently Asked Questions
URL: https://dining.umd.edu/about-us/faqs

Q: How many dining halls does UMD have?
A: UMD has three resident dining halls: 251 North Dining Hall, South Campus Dining Hall, and Yahentamitsi Dining Hall.

Q: What are the dining hall hours?
A: Yahentamitsi and South Campus Dining Halls are open Monday through Friday 7am to 9pm. On Saturdays and Sundays they open at 10am and close at 9pm. 251 North Dining Hall closes at 7pm on Fridays through Sundays.

Q: Are all dining halls open on weekends?
A: Yes, all three dining halls are open on weekends during the school year, though with slightly adjusted hours.

Q: Which dining hall is the main one?
A: There is no official main dining hall. Students tend to use whichever is closest to their dorm. Yahentamitsi is the newest and most popular. 251 North stays open the latest on weekdays.

Q: Can I use my meal plan at all three dining halls?
A: Yes, meal swipes can be used at all three resident dining halls.

Q: How does UMD accommodate food allergies?
A: UMD Dining has a Purple Zone at 251 North and South Campus Dining Hall that is free from the eight most common allergens. Each hall also has a Purple Freezer with pre-packaged allergen-labeled items. Students with allergies should fill out the allergy/intolerance form and meet with the nutrition team.

Q: Is there vegan and vegetarian food available?
A: Yes. All dining halls offer vegan and vegetarian options daily. Yahentamitsi has a dedicated Good Food station. Menu items are labeled with dietary icons. Students can filter menus by dietary preference at nutrition.umd.edu.

Q: How do I know if food is vegetarian or vegan?
A: All menu items are labeled at the counter and on the online menu at nutrition.umd.edu. If you are ever unsure, ask the manager or chef on duty rather than front-line servers.

Q: Does UMD offer halal food options?
A: Students with halal dietary requirements can contact Dining Services to discuss options via the halal diet form on the dining website.

Q: What should I do if I have a severe food allergy?
A: Fill out the allergy/intolerance form on the dining website. Always carry your Epi-Pen. Ask the manager or chef on duty for ingredient questions — not front-line servers. Each dining hall has an Epi-Pen on site.

Q: What if I am sick and cannot go to the dining hall?
A: UMD Dining offers a Sick Meal service for resident students. Contact your RA or housing staff to initiate a sick meal request.

Q: Where can I find nutritional information for dining hall food?
A: Detailed nutritional information for all menu items is available at nutrition.umd.edu.
